Message from Secretary General

Hello,
As we get ready to usher in the New Year with great fanfare and celebration, it is natural that we look forward to 2017 being the harbinger of a new phase of success, prosperity and well-being in our lives. In the same vein, a group of young and vibrant CETians takes great honour to be a part of an endeavour that you are sure to cherish- CET MUN 2017. Being one of the oldest MUNs in Odisha whose first edition was organised way back in 2013, CET MUN pioneered the healthy culture of MUNs in this circuit. Now, its back in full vigour and in a brand new Avatar. Spread over three days of stimulating discussions and dialogue, the three committees being simulated in this edition are sure to define constructive diplomacy. It is our abiding commitment to ensure quality of debate and provide a grand platform to over 250 delegates to articulate themselves and prove their skills. Our Secretariat constantly strives to offer a memorable experience- be it within the council or outside it. The council proceedings apart, the Delegates should look forward to multiple social nights with the star night sure to take everybody's breath away! Too much revelation for now! As the conference approaches, we would be giving you a thousand reasons to honour us and enrich yourselves with your presence. Once again, a Hearty Welcome to CET MUN 2017. We just can't wait to have you here soon!

United Nation General Assembly DISEC

Agenda :

Armed Conflict in Darfur-Possible solutions to the Hostilities.
Strength: 150 Delegates
Executive Board:Chairperson: Ms. Soumya Mohapatra
Vice Chairperson: Mr. Anindya Misra

This is the first committee of the General Assembly which has the mandate to debate and discuss within the specialised domain of 'Disarmament and Security'. In accordance with the powers conferred to it and within the ambit of the UN Charter, The First Committee deliberates upon global security issues and imminent challenges to world peace. Its solutions are advisory in nature.

United Nations General Assembly ECOSOC

Agenda :

Economic Development and Livelihood for victims of European Migrant Crisis.
Strength: 60 Delegates
EExecutive Board: Joint- Presidents: Mr. Ipsit Bibhudarshi and Mr. Abhradeep Mukherjee

The UN-ECOSOC functions as the grand integrator of the United Nations' efforts directed at promoting sustainable development- be it social, economic and environmental. Discussions in the ECOSOC focus on suggesting innovative ways to promote economic growth that is socially compatible and environmentally sustainable. The resolutions are purely advisory in nature.

United Nations Human Rights Commission

Agenda :

Reviewing the status of rights of sexual minorities: LGBTI
Strength: 70 Delegates
Executive Board: Chairperson: Mr. Vaivab Das and Vice Chairperson: Ms. Suravi Patnaik

The Human Rights Council draws its inspiration and objectives from a document that serves as the veritable holy book upholding the principle of human rights- the Human Rights Charter. In the words of UN Secretary General Ban Ki-Moon, “All victims of human rights abuses should be able to look to the Human Rights Council as a forum and a springboard for action.”

About Us

Spread across a lush green expanse of around 100 acres near the historical Khandagiri and Udaygiri cave monuments, College of Engineering and Technology, Bhubaneswar, proudly takes its place amongst the best government engineering colleges of Odisha. Established in 1981 , CETB has been known to churn out some of the best minds and highly placed intellectuals and personalities in various walks of life since its inception. The college boasts of numerous clubs, societies and chapters spanning different fields and departments. There are many events and fests that have allured swarming, energetic and ever increasing crowds from various colleges both within and outside Odisha, the notable one among them being the CET MUN, which includes 3 committees with more than 250 delegates in attendance, and promises to be the next big thing in town!
